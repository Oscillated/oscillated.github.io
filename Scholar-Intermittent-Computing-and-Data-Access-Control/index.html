IoT devices are of huge diversities in capabilities, sizes and application domains.

Their hardware architectures and their operating systems would often need very different design concepts.

two major designs issues in ultra-light-weighted devices that might operate in an intermittent fashion:

  • Task Scheduling
  • Concurrency Control in Data Access

trade-off between

  • running time of tasks
  • the system performance

Autonomous I/O for Intermittent IoT Systems

Self-powered intermittent systems waste considerable I/O energy

  • volatile I/O modules repeatedly issue identical operations under power failure conditions
  • the use of the inefficient I/O stack originally developed for battery-powered systems.

Autonomous I/O

  • accumulatively and transparently complete I/O operations regardless of power stability.

  • two essential functionalities

    • separate the general I/O stack to make accumulatively-completed I/O operations transparent to application tasks
    • propose an access protocol that allows for energy efficiency and compatibility with the general I/O stack.
