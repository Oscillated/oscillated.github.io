Internet of Things (IoT) can be defined as

  • the network of physical objects augmented with electronics, software, sensors, and connectivity to enable objects to acquire data, and exchange data with data with servers, centralized systems, and/or other connected devices based on a variety of communication infrastructure
  • When IoT is augmented with sensors and actuators, IoT is able to support cyber-physical applications by which networked objects can impact the physical environment by executing “physical” actions.

Cyber Attack of IoT

  • because of its fine-grained, continuous and pervasive data acquisition, and control and actuation capabilities, and the lack of adequate security, IoT expands the cyber attack surface
  • IoT systems may not have a well defined perimeters
  • because of mobility, they continuously change
  • IoT devices are highly heterogeneous with respect to communication capabilities and protocols,and software and hardware platforms
  • IoT systems may include physically unprotected portions
  • In addition, IoT systems may include objects not designed to be connected to the Internet, thus they may not have any security capabilities
  • Industrial IoT (IIoT) and critical infrastructures IoT systems have additional risks
    • the long lifecycle of systems
    • the need for continuous operations
    • outdated or nonexistent policy and access management

a major challenge that needs to be addressed is how to protect against safety risks arising from security vulnerabilities

  • the physical behavior requirements that IoT devices may have to comply with, may offer attackers new opportunities to undermine safety.
  • because of being tightly integrated with the physical environment, IoT systems can be the target of cyber-physical attacks that by manipulating the physical space can prevent IoT devices from working properly

the most important challenge is security administration

  • approaches by which IoT devices and systems can automatically administer security with little or not help by end-users.
  • a promising approach based on the notion of community-wiki for devices to provide security configuration policies that devices can understand and enforce.
